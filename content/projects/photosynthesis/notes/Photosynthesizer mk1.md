## Build Guide

### Legs x2
- Cut and straighten 440mm  of 2mm Brass Rod and cut in to 2 x 220mm
- make bends
- solder braces
- even out leg lengths

Motor Mount Connections x 2
- cut and straighten 220mm 1.5mm brass rod and cut into 2 x 100mm or so
- bend 180 hook at with about 5mm length on the short end
- align in motor mount and frame, and bend front arm down 90 degrees
- chop excess off front arms
- mark upright connections through motor mount
- solder uprights
- Break contacts (see pics)
- epoxy connections in place

- Manufacturer: Often associated with Mabuchi Motor, Japan
- Rated Voltage: 6 V DC (can operate from around 3 V to 12 V)
- No-load Speed: Approximately 2400 RPM
- Rotation: Usually available in clockwise (CW) or counter-clockwise (CCW) variants
- Type: Coreless or precious metal brush motor designed for stable speed control
- Size: Typical diameter ~32mm, length ~23mm, shaft diameter 2mm, shaft length ~8mm
- Weight: Approx. 70g
- Application: Used as a capstan motor in tape decks, audio spindle motor, and other precision speed stabilizing applications
- Additional features: Some versions include internal speed stabilizing circuits and trimmer potentiometers for adjustment

The motor is recognized for smooth, low-noise operation with steady rotational speed, suited for audio playback devices where precise speed control is critical.[](https://www.networkhardwares.com/en-bh/products/eg-530ad-dc-motor-6-9-12v-forward-reverse-motor-cw-ccw-up-to-2400rpm-recorder-motor)

## Similar Low-Noise Motors

Look for **coreless** or **precious metal brush** motors for lowest noise and smoothest operation.

- **Mabuchi RS-360SH**  
    A popular small DC motor often used in tape decks and small appliances. It offers smooth operation with lower noise and is available in various voltages (3V to 12V). Often used in hobby and audio applications.
    
- **Maxon RE-MAX Series**  
    Precision coreless motors designed for low noise and high efficiency, common in medical, robotics, and instrumentation fields. These motors run smoothly and quietly with excellent speed control.
    
- **Faulhaber 1724 Series Coreless Motor**  
    Known for ultra-low noise and vibration, coreless construction, suited for audio, medical, and instrumentation. Typically more expensive but offers superior performance.
    
- **Johnson Electric RE/RS Series**  
    Used in small appliances and instrumentation; these motors are designed for quiet operation, available in multiple voltages including low voltage variants.
    
- **Pololu 100:1 Metal Gearmotor with 6V or 12V Options**  
    Gearmotors with metal gearing, optimized for low noise and stable torque; suitable if reduced speed and steady output torque are needed.
    
- **Nidec 130 and 180 Series**  
    These are compact precision motors often chosen for audio, disc drives, and similar applications with noise-sensitive requirements.

## Pulley Selection
For a solar-powered DC motor driving a small load with pulleys spaced 100–150 mm apart and a 3:1 speed reduction (45 mm/15 mm pulley diameters), several timing belt options—including MXL, XL, GT2, HTD, and T-series—are available, each with unique properties and suitability for your scenario.[](https://www.sdp-si.com/D265/PDF/D265P1003.pdf)



## Belt Types and Suitability

- **MXL and XL (Trapezoidal profile):**
    - These legacy designs are suited for low-power, precise motion applications, and may be sufficient for your solar-powered, low-torque motor. XL belts are a bit larger (~5.08 mm pitch vs. 2.03 mm for MXL), usually offering higher capacity and working tension.[](https://uk.misumi-ec.com/vona2/detail/110302566050/)

- These belts typically have higher backlash than modern profiles and are popular in small robotics and light-duty mechanisms.[](https://www.york-ind.com/blog/timing-belts/understanding-belt-profiles-powergrip-gt2-gt3-htd-mxl-xl-l-40-dp-a-and-at)

- **GT2 and HTD (Curvilinear/high-torque profiles):**
    
    - GT2 belts are designed for better engagement and lower backlash, especially with low tension and small pulleys—ideal for precise motion and lighter applications.[](https://reprap.org/forum/read.php?14%2C423480)

- HTD belts are rated for higher torque and are robust for more demanding loads, with slightly higher backlash than GT2, but smoother running.[](https://www.chiefdelphi.com/t/htd-vs-gt2-belt-for-crescendo-shooter/467430)
    
- ​
    
- Both offer improved torque transfer and longevity over trapezoidal profiles, and tend to be fiber reinforced for minimal stretch.[](https://www.chiefdelphi.com/t/belt-types/393986)
    

- - ​
        
- **T-Series:**
    
    - T-series (T2.5, T5, etc.) are common in European drives, featuring trapezoidal teeth; performance is similar to XL and may suit precision/small loads.
        

## Recommended Profile for Your Application

For low-power solar drive applications and small to moderate speeds:

- **GT2 recommended:** The GT2 profile offers optimal engagement, minimal backlash (important for slow speed, low-tension), and is widely available in small widths for short center distances.[](https://www.york-ind.com/blog/timing-belts/understanding-belt-profiles-powergrip-gt2-gt3-htd-mxl-xl-l-40-dp-a-and-at)
    

- ​
    
- **XL or T-series are also acceptable** if price or availability is a deciding factor, but expect more backlash (slightly less smooth, especially with plastic or 3D-printed pulleys).[](https://www.tyma.eu/products/timing-belts/drive/inch-sizes/xl/)
    
- ​
    
- **Avoid HTD** unless you expect higher torque or need heavy-duty reliability, as it's typically oversized and more expensive for simple small-load applications.[](https://www.chiefdelphi.com/t/htd-vs-gt2-belt-for-crescendo-shooter/467430)
    

- ​
    

## Sizing and Selection

- For a 100–150 mm center distance and a 3:1 pulley ratio, all profiles (MXL, XL, GT2, T-series) can be sourced in suitable lengths, and pulleys matching your diameter are readily available for each profile.[](https://sg.misumi-ec.com/vona2/detail/110310459179/)
    

- ​
    
- Use fiber-reinforced belts for minimal stretch, especially for solar applications where stability is important.[](https://www.chiefdelphi.com/t/belt-types/393986)
    
- ​
    
- Keep tooth count on the small pulley at least 12–16, as smaller counts can lead to excessive wear and noise, especially in GT2 or HTD belts.[](https://reprap.org/forum/read.php?14%2C423480)
